# Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare

Artificial intelligence (AI) is increasingly used across healthcare. Machine
learning models analyze medical images such as X-rays, CT scans, and MRIs to
detect conditions like tumors, fractures, and diabetic retinopathy, often
matching or exceeding the accuracy of human specialists in narrow tasks.

## Diagnostics

Deep learning systems trained on large labeled datasets can flag suspicious
lesions in radiology and pathology. These tools act as a second reader,
reducing missed diagnoses and prioritizing urgent cases in the queue.

## Drug Discovery

AI accelerates drug discovery by predicting molecular properties and protein
structures. Models like AlphaFold predict 3D protein folding, helping
researchers identify promising drug targets faster and at lower cost than
traditional laboratory methods.

## Clinical Workflow

Natural language processing extracts structured information from unstructured
clinical notes, automating documentation and coding. Predictive models
estimate patient deterioration risk, sepsis onset, and hospital readmission,
enabling earlier intervention.

## Risks and Limitations

AI in healthcare raises concerns about bias, since models trained on
non-representative data may underperform for some populations. Other challenges
include data privacy, the need for regulatory approval, lack of
interpretability in deep models, and the requirement for clinical validation
before deployment. Human oversight remains essential.
